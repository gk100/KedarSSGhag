Forecasting Water Levels in the Everglades using Artificial Neural Networks

Research Project      M.Sc. Hydroinformatics

Forecasting of water level at various locations of rivers and canals in the Everglades (Florida) is vital for flood, drought, and ecological modelling. The Everglades is a data rich environment and it is proposed to test the usefulness of data driven models (DDM) in this environment. An additional challenge will be to use ensemble rainfall prediction from numerical weather models in 4 data-driven models to provide useful information from data-driven models. The main objective of the study will be to build data-driven models using artificial neural network based on past water level data and forecast rainfall from numerical weather models to predict water level at various locations of the Everglades.
